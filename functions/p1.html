Lady In Red

Rags.
Red eyes.
Scream.
Sudden rainfall.
Road side.
I was driving my car late at night, through the isolated highway,
Listening to the radio, I heard someone screaming.

Brakes.
Skid.
Shriek.
Stepped out.
Road side.

A chill ran down my spine as I heard whispering among the damp leaves,
My Jaws had dropped and my eyes were filled with a fog of disbelief.

Blood.
Blood.
Blood.

Was it blood or her red dress?
Her face had scars and legs were covered in mud.
As she dragged herself away from the roadside
A shiver swiped throughout my body.

I had seen the worst,
But I almost held my breath, as the worst was yet to come
I walked closer to her slowly,
Huh! OMG a rape again.

Hush.
Distant howl.
Sudden gust.
Again hush.

When I reached her,
She had tears in her eyes as blood poured from her body,
Leaning on the odd side, she sighed!

Tears.
Trauma.
Pain.
Blood.
Another victim.
Of sexual assault.

Poem By::

Mayank Kanware
